Stroke Awareness & Education Hub

A stroke is a medical emergency that occurs when blood flow to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients. Brain cells begin to die in minutes.

The BEFAST Medical Framework

B - Balance

Watch for sudden dizziness, loss of coordination, or unsteadiness while trying to stand up or walk.

E - Eyes

Check for sudden trouble seeing out of one or both eyes, blurry vision, or sudden double vision layouts.

F - Face

Ask the individual to smile. Look closely to see if one side of their mouth or face droops downward.

A - Arms

Ask them to lift up both arms. Check if one arm automatically drifts downwards or feels weak and numb.

S - Speech

Have them repeat a short, simple phrase. Note if their speech sounds slurred, slow, or hard to follow.

T - Time

If any single one of these symptoms is observed, it is immediately time to call 911 emergency care.

First Aid Protocol While Waiting for Help

What to DO ✅ What NOT to Do ❌
Call 911 instantly. Note down the exact time you first saw a symptom surface. Do NOT offer medication. Aspirin can make hemorrhagic (bleeding) strokes worse.
Keep the person lying down comfortably on their side to optimize respiration pathways. Do NOT let them sleep. Symptoms do not get better or reset through sleep.
Reassure them and remain right by their side until the emergency vehicles arrive. Do NOT give them food or water. Strokes weaken swallowing, causing choking risks.
